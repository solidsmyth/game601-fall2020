My games from GAME 601 - Game Prototyping at American University in Fall 2020

Game Modification: Loyalty or Treachery
“Loyalty or Treachery” is a modified and expanded version of the card game “Red or Black.” Two players face off against one another by drawing from the same deck, guessing the color of the card prior to drawing. Guessing correctly means they have the chance to manipulate the state of the game. Guessing incorrectly means they pass that chance, and card, over to the other player. When the main deck is gone, the player with the most cards wins.

(Midterm) Non-Digital Game: Engine's of War
Engine's of War is a 2-4 (currently only 2) player strategy card game. Player's battle each other by drawing cards from the same deck and playing Faction cards with different abilities. Players discard cards in order to play other cards using the games unique Faction Feeding system. The game is split into three phases: The Battle Phase, the Negotiation Phase, and the Organization Phase. These three phases break up play to allow for maximum strategic choice and tactical decision making. Players win by earning enough Victory Points or by fulfilling a Factions Main Objective. Will you defeat you opponents army, who will you fall under the might of their Engine of War?

Interactive Fiction: Irregularities
Irregularities is a narrative fiction game developed in Twine. In Irregularities, the player is presented with basic questions that they must answer, but there are other answers already put in goading the player into choosing them and sending them on a different path. Will you choose to follow the rules, or embrace irregularities?

Programming with p5js: Save Gary!
Gary is being thrown into the lava. Using your mouse, click on him and keep him out of the lava. But remember, the more you save him, the faster he is being thrown into the lava!!

(Final) Digital Game: The System
The System is game of narrative choice developed in Twine. The player is an individual who discovers that they have been chosen by an AI called The System to answer a series of questions regarding humanity. The player also explores the room that The System resides in and can discover information about humanities past. With music, sound effects, branching choices, and multiple endings, The System offers a small but great narrative experience for players.
